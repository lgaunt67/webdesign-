- CHILDREN IN NEED -


- What is Children in Need raising money for? -

BBC Children in Need raises money to support disadvantaged children and young people across the UK, funding local charities and community projects that help with poverty, mental health, disabilities, crisis support, and safe spaces, ensuring children get essential items, emotional support, and opportunities to thrive.



- How much money has been raised? -

Over the years, BBC Children in Need has raised millions of pounds through donations from the public, corporate partners, and special events. Major contributors include Asda, McDonald’s, and the Post Office. Altogether, the charity has raised over £1 billion since its first appeal in 1980.



- Local events happening in Aberdeen -

Several charity and fundraising events are taking place in Aberdeen in 2025 that support children’s causes, including a BBC Children in Need walk by the OneStop Community Team, the Archie Foundation’s Disco Ball at the Chester Hotel in August, and the Aberdeen Kiltwalk in June, which raises money for children and families across the North of Scotland. There is also a Stay & Play under-5 Halloween fundraiser happening in October.
